About Me

I started programming in 2018 with C and C++, exploring logic and structure through small console projects. Later, I learned Java after getting inspired by Minecraft during the lockdown, which made me curious about how games are built. That eventually led me to create Android apps with Java, and later with Kotlin using Jetpack Compose.

During my diploma, I learned Python and completed an internship where I built several small projects, including a music player and some simple games. Over time, I explored Git, GitHub, and full-stack development with Firebase, Supabase, and Vercel. My interest later shifted toward Artificial Intelligence, where I started experimenting with LLMs, image generation, video generation, ASR, and TTS. I currently focus on testing these systems locally and offline.
