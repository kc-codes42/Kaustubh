About Me

I first got drawn into computers around 2016, fascinated by how software seemed to “think.” By 2018, I started programming seriously. My first real dive was into C and C++, exploring logic and structure through small console projects.

In 2019, I began building small utilities and experimenting with Android development. My first Android app was a simple note-taking tool, basic but it worked. Around the same time, I also started learning how the Internet works at a deeper level, how data flows, how systems connect.

In 2020, I joined GitHub and began publishing early experiments, some successful, some forgotten. During the pandemic, I spent months studying UI/UX design and experimenting with how software feels, not just how it functions.

In 2021, I moved to structured learning, exploring the intersection of Software Engineering and Artificial Intelligence. I studied Algorithms, Operating Systems, and the core of how machines execute abstract logic. I also started working with the Jetpack Compose ecosystem, which later became my main Android framework.

2022 was the year I began combining AI and development. I built automation tools, tested prompt-based architectures, and explored how natural language can shape computation. This curiosity evolved into a focused pursuit, mastering artificial intelligence.

In 2023, I began a two-year plan for AI mastery, combining theoretical study with practical projects. I worked on a timetable builder using Jetpack Compose and Firestore, a cross-platform college app using React Native and Supabase, and several analytics systems integrating with Supabase and Firebase.

I balance technical work with interests in sound design, anime aesthetics, and digital minimalism. I often experiment with distorted soundscapes and retro-tech-inspired visuals, drawing from media such as Serial Experiments Lain.

Currently, I study Computer Science and Engineering, preparing for the GATE exam while refining my AI engineering and full-stack development skills.
